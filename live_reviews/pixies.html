Pixies

The Barbican, York 20/05/2026

When I first became aware of the Pixies thanks to a school friend lending me Doolittle around 1996/97, if you’d have told me I’d be seeing them in a venue in the city I lived almost 39 years later, I’d have laughed in your face. Such was the band’s mythical status after their breakup in 1993 that any reformation, let alone a tour taking in York, would’ve been crazy talk. Yet here we are - the 20th May 2026, and the band are playing at The Barbican as part of their 40th anniversary tour.

Waiting for them to take the stage, I was wondering if the Pixies had ever played York before... it seemed unlikely. Helpfully, Black Francis (aka. Frank Black) confirms this as he greets the crowd before leading the band through a chilled version of the Doolittle classic Wave of Mutilation. This is indeed the band’s first trip to our great city, but they hope to be back soon. 


The set that follows is an eclectic mix of classics, drawn mainly from Doolittle, Come On Pilgrim and Surfer Rosa, with a healthy smattering of tracks from 2024’s The Night The Zombies Came. Whilst never a particularly active band on stage, the Pixies are engaging nonetheless, with a few tricks up their sleeve: lead guitarist Joey Santiago plays using his baseball cap at one point and also summons a wash of unholy noise using his unplugged guitar cord and some choice effects pedals. 

I’ve only ever caught the Pixies live once before, at Hyde Park a few years ago, but I can’t imagine tonight sounds much different from their live show back in the late 80s/early 90s. There might not be quite as much screaming from Black (there’s still a fair bit - Tame being a good example), but drums, bass, guitar and melodic vocals are all totally on point. New bassist Emma Richardson does a great job of covering the female backing, and occasional lead, vocals, originally performed by Kim Deal. To be honest, I’d be hard pressed to tell if the likes of Bone Machine, Debaser or Head On were performed in 2026 or 1990-something.
Overall, the set is very solid. From a personal standpoint, I was pleased to hear Planet of Sound and Motorway to Roswell, two of the deeper cuts from Trompe le Monde, my favourite Pixies album, but I can't imagine too many fans will have complaints about the content. Let's hope Black Francis is true to this word and the Pixies return to York in the not too distant future.
